Dim sum, a staple of Cantonese cuisine, also embodies the communal and culinary richness of Hong Kong. Traditionally enjoyed as a morning or early afternoon meal, dim sum involves small, bite-sized portions of food served in steamer baskets or on small plates, including a variety of dumplings, buns, and rolls. This tradition not only showcases the culinary artistry of Cantonese cooking but also encourages a shared dining experience that is integral to Hong Kong's social fabric.
